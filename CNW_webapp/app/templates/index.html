About the Project:

This application serves as a proof of concept for the feasability of using machine learning to assist volunteers for the Conservation Northwest Wildlife Monitoring Project with the identification of animals in remote camera images.

If successful, a similar model could be applied to detecting "false triggers" - images with no animal. Automatically flagging or removing false triggers from project photo sets has the potential to save the organization hundreds of volunteer hours every year!

What it Does:

The Wildlife Identification Web app will identify photos you upload! Based on images it has already seen, the model places photos into one of five categories: Canine, Feline, Ungulate (deer, elk), Small, and Other (Human, Bear, Wolverine, ect.).

Learn more about the technology and process behind this project on github

Special thanks to everyone who made this project possible including: Conservation Northwest for the images used in model training; David Moskowitz for guidance and support in creating the project and providing the initial inspiration; and to the instructional staff at the Galvanize Seattle, Data Science Immersive Program for sharing their incredible knowledge and expertise.

Identification Web app


IDENTIFY YOUR OWN IMAGES!


The web app is intended for proof of concept and entertainment. Because the initial model was trained on a very small set of backgrounds, it is imprecise on general photos.

You have been warned!

Clear images of species seen during model training work best - coyote, bobcat, elk, and such. Your family dog may come back classified as an Ungulate. Have fun and let me know what you think.
